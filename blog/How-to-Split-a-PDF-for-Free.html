How to Split a PDF for Free
— Without Uploading Your File

Extract pages, ranges, and more in your browser. No account, no server, no watermark. Works on desktop and mobile in under 30 seconds.

On this page
    TL;DR

    Go to ihatepdf.cv/split-pdf, drop in your PDF, choose your split mode, and download. Done in under 30 seconds — no sign-up needed.

    Why people need to split PDFs

    PDF splitting is one of those tasks that sounds simple but catches people off guard. You receive a 90-page contract and only need page 14. Your thesis is 120 pages but the submission portal has a 5MB cap. A scanned invoice batch has 40 separate bills merged into one file.

    These are the most common reasons people search for a way to split a PDF for free:

    • A government or university portal rejects the file because it's too large
    • You only want to share specific pages with a client or colleague — not the whole document
    • Invoices, receipts, or contracts were batch-scanned into one PDF and need separating
    • You want to extract a single chapter from a long report or eBook
    • A double-sided scan mixed odd and even pages that need separating before reordering

    Whatever your reason, the process should take seconds — and it does, with the right tool.

    How to split a PDF free — step by step

    Using ihatepdf.cv's split tool requires no installation, no login, and no payment. Here's exactly what to do:

    1. Open the tool — Navigate to ihatepdf.cv/split-pdf. No sign-up required, it loads immediately.
    2. Upload your PDF — Click the upload area or drag and drop your file. The file is loaded into your browser's memory only.
    3. Choose your split mode — Select whether you want to split every page, extract a range, extract specific pages, or separate odd and even pages.
    4. Click Split PDF — Processing happens locally via WebAssembly. For most files, this takes just a few seconds.
    5. Download your result — Single-range exports download as a PDF. Multi-file exports download as a ZIP archive. No watermarks — ever.

    Privacy note: ihatepdf.cv processes your file using pdf-lib via WebAssembly — a browser technology that runs code locally, the same way a native app would. Your PDF bytes are never transmitted to any external server. Open your browser's Network tab in DevTools while using the tool and you'll see zero file data leaving your machine.

    The four split modes explained

    Not all PDF splitting tasks are the same. ihatepdf.cv offers four distinct modes depending on what you need:

    Mode 01
    Split every page
    Breaks the PDF into one file per page. All resulting files are bundled into a ZIP download. Perfect for distributing pages individually or archiving each page separately.
    Mode 02
    Extract page range
    Enter a range like 3–7 to pull out pages 3 through 7 as a single PDF. Enter multiple ranges like 1–3, 8–10 to extract different sections as one combined file.
    Mode 03
    Extract specific pages
    Enter individual page numbers like 1, 4, 9 to pull out just those pages — even non-consecutive ones — into a new PDF.
    Mode 04
    Split odd / even pages
    Ideal for double-sided scans where front and back pages need separating before being recombined in the correct order.

    How to extract just one page

    This is one of the most searched PDF tasks online — and it's simpler than most people expect. In the Extract page range mode on ihatepdf.cv, enter the same number in both the start and end fields. For example, entering 5 (or 5–5) extracts only page 5 as a standalone PDF file.

    You can also use the Extract specific pages mode and enter a single page number. Either approach produces a clean single-page PDF with no quality loss and no re-encoding.

    How to extract non-consecutive pages

    Say you need pages 1, 3, 5, 8, and 12 from a 40-page report — skipping everything else. In the Extract specific pages mode, simply enter:

    1, 3, 5, 8, 12

    ihatepdf.cv will assemble exactly those pages, in the order specified, into a new PDF. You can also mix ranges and individual pages: 1, 3, 5–8, 12 is perfectly valid and will extract pages 1, 3, 5, 6, 7, 8, and 12.

    Splitting vs. deleting — what's the difference?

    Splitting extracts pages into a new file. It doesn't remove pages from the original. If your goal is to delete specific pages and keep the rest, you need a different workflow.

    For that, use the Organize Pages tool on ihatepdf.cv. It shows thumbnail previews of every page, lets you click a delete icon on any page you want to remove, and then downloads the cleaned PDF — with all remaining pages intact and in order. You can also rotate sideways or upside-down pages in the same step.

    How to split a PDF to meet file size limits

    Government portals, university submission forms, and job application systems frequently impose file size limits — typically between 2MB and 10MB. A 50-page scanned PDF can easily exceed that. Here's the most reliable workflow:

    1. First, try compressing the PDF at ihatepdf.cv/compress-pdf. Compression alone often reduces file size by 60–80% without any visible quality loss.
    2. If the compressed file is still too large, split the document into logical sections — for example, chapters 1–3 in one file and chapters 4–6 in another.
    3. Compress each section separately to bring each one under the portal's limit.
    4. Submit each section individually, or merge them back together at ihatepdf.cv/merge-pdf once the size restriction no longer applies.

    Pro tip: Scanned PDFs are almost always larger than native digital PDFs. If your document originated as a scan, compressing it first — before splitting — will save you significant time and reduce the number of parts you need to submit.

    Does splitting affect PDF quality?

    No. Splitting is a purely structural operation. When ihatepdf.cv splits your file, it does not re-encode, re-compress, or render any page content. The bytes that make up each page are copied verbatim into the new file.

    ElementAfter splitting
    Fonts & textIdentical — vector data is copied exactly
    Images & photosPreserved at original resolution, no re-encoding
    Hyperlinks & annotationsPreserved within extracted pages
    Embedded metadataRetained per-page
    Document-level bookmarksMay not resolve if target page is not included

    This is worth emphasising because some online PDF tools — especially those that route your file through a server-side rendering pipeline — can silently degrade image quality or strip metadata. Since ihatepdf.cv uses pdf-lib locally in your browser, that pipeline simply doesn't exist.

    Frequently asked questions

    Is there a page limit when splitting PDFs on ihatepdf.cv?
    No. You can split PDFs with hundreds of pages. The only practical constraint is your device's available RAM, since the file is loaded into browser memory. In practice, even a 500-page PDF processes without issues on most modern laptops and phones.
    Can I use it on my phone?
    Yes. ihatepdf.cv works on mobile browsers — Chrome, Safari, Firefox, and Edge on both Android and iOS. The interface is mobile-optimised and the file picker integrates with your phone's Files app, Google Drive, and iCloud Drive.
    What happens to my file after I'm done?
    Nothing — because it was never sent anywhere. Your file exists only in your browser's memory tab, and is cleared when you close or refresh the page. ihatepdf.cv has no access to it at any point.
    Can I split a password-protected PDF?
    If the PDF requires a password to open, you'll need to enter that password in your PDF viewer first and save an unlocked copy. Use ihatepdf.cv/remove-password to strip the restriction, then split.
    Does splitting a PDF preserve hyperlinks, bookmarks, and metadata?
    Yes. All page content — including embedded hyperlinks, fonts, and image data — is preserved exactly. Document-level bookmarks that reference pages not included in a given split may no longer resolve, but the page content itself is untouched.
    Is ihatepdf.cv completely free?
    Yes. All tools on ihatepdf.cv are free to use without any account, subscription, or usage cap. There are no watermarks added to your files.
